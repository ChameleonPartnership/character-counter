Frequently Asked Questions

How do I count characters in text?

Simply paste or type your text into the input box at the top. Character counts update in real-time, showing both characters with and without spaces. Our tool instantly counts all characters, including letters, numbers, spaces, and symbols.

What's the difference between characters and words?

Characters include every letter, number, space, and symbol. Words are counted as sequences separated by spaces. For example, 'Hello world' is 11 characters (including the space) but only 2 words.

What are platform character limits used for?

Different platforms enforce different character limits optimised for their format and user experience. Twitter allows 280 per post, LinkedIn allows 3,000 for deeper storytelling, email subject lines are optimal at 50 characters, and SEO page titles should stay under 60 characters for full display in search results.

How is reading time calculated?

Reading time is estimated by dividing your text's total word count by the selected words-per-minute (WPM) rate. The default is 250 WPM (average reader), but you can adjust to 200 WPM (slow readers) or 300 WPM (fast readers) depending on text complexity and audience.

What's the best character count to aim for on Twitter?

Twitter's limit is 280 characters. For maximum engagement and easy retweets, aim for 100–260 characters — long enough to convey your message completely, but short enough to allow space for replies and comments from followers.

How do I optimise my LinkedIn post length?

LinkedIn posts can be up to 3,000 characters. For optimal engagement, aim for 1,500–2,500 characters — long enough to tell a compelling story with personality and details, but short enough to keep readers engaged without requiring them to click 'see more'.

Why do email subject lines have a 50-character limit?

Email clients display approximately 40–50 characters on mobile devices and 60 on desktop. Subject lines longer than 50 characters are truncated on mobile, which reduces visibility and can lower click-through rates. Keep them at 30–50 characters for optimal display across devices.

What's the ideal length for an SEO meta description?

Google displays 150–160 characters on desktop and 120 characters on mobile. Write meta descriptions between 150–160 characters to ensure they display fully in search results, and include your primary keyword naturally within that space.

Can I count special characters separately?

Yes. Click 'Show Details' under your text to see a complete breakdown including uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, spaces, and special characters counted separately. This is useful for password requirements and form validation rules.

What counts as a 'word' in your counter?

A word is any sequence of characters separated by spaces or line breaks. Numbers and hyphenated words (e.g., 'well-structured') each count as single words. Standalone punctuation marks do not count as words.

Yes, Character Counter Pro is fully responsive and works on all devices including smartphones and tablets. Simply visit the site on your mobile browser and start typing — all features work exactly the same on desktop, tablet, and mobile.
